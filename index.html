Neptune

The Solar System's Windiest Planet

The Blue Giant

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the most distant in our solar system. It’s a cold, windy gas giant with a striking blue color due to methane. Neptune is about 17 times heavier than Earth and takes 165 years to complete one orbit around the Sun.

Discovery Through Mathematics

Unlike planets known since ancient times, Neptune was discovered through calculations. In 1846, scientists noticed Uranus’s orbit was being disturbed. French mathematician Urbain Le Verrier predicted Neptune’s position, and German astronomer Johann Galle confirmed it.

Strong Winds

Neptune is known for its extreme weather, with winds reaching over 2,000 kilometers per hour — the fastest in the solar system.

Moons

Discover Neptune's 14 Satellites

Triton – The Captured Giant

Of Neptune's 14 known moons, Triton is largest, orbiting in the opposite direction of the planet’s rotation. It likely came from the Kuiper Belt. With icy geysers and a thin atmosphere, it’s geologically active.

Proteus – The Dark, Misshapen World

Proteus is Neptune’s second-largest moon. It has an irregular shape, a dark, cratered surface, and shows no geological activity.

Nereid – The Wild Orbiter

Nereid has a highly elliptical orbit around Neptune, possibly due to gravitational disruption. It's about 340 kilometers wide and likely composed of ice and rock, though it remains largely unexplored.

Larissa – The Hidden Inner Moon

Larissa orbits close to Neptune and has an irregular, cratered surface. Discovered by Voyager 2, it lies within Neptune’s rings and is affected by the planet’s strong gravity.
